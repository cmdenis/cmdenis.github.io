Coarse Graining

We are exposed every day to the idea of scale and coarse-graining, possibly without even realizing it. Taking a picture with a phone inevitably coarse-grains the image we see before our eyes in the form of pixels. A similar, more quantitative example could be the process of averaging all the grades of students in a class: we are grouping each grain (students) into a coarser grain (the class), and then focusing on the new grains (classes).

Coarse-graining can be desired for many reasons. One of which is the way it deals with information. It often allows us to compress things in a way that better reveals the "useful" dynamics of the system without having to worry about too many moving pieces. Many more abstract examples exist in physics, typically taking different names.
