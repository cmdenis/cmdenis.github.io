Entrainment

Entrainment is this phenomenon in which some oscillator starts syncing up to the frequency of an external oscillator. This is similar to a musician syncing up to the beat of a metronome. The metronome's rhythm will remain unchanged, however, the musician will do their best to follow the rhythm of the metronome.

A more general phenomenon, but very related, is that of synchronization, where two or more oscillators start matching their frequencies together. This kind of phenomenon has been shown (in an almost cliché way) to arise in so many situations. Examples include the syncing up of fireflies' pulses to crowds clapping.
